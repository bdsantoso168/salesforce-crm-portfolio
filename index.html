01 / The Challenge

Three constraints. One failed pilot.

Every stakeholder pulled in a different direction, and the last attempt had already burned the room's trust.

VP of Sales
Wants speed

Reps need to close faster. Any solution that slows the pipeline is dead on arrival.

IT Director
Requires SAP integration

Order and inventory data must flow from SAP before go-live, no exceptions.

CFO
Holds a fixed budget

No open cheque. Scope has to fit a hard number without a phase-two rescue.

✕
The pilot that failed

A prior CRM rollout collapsed on contact with the sales floor. Reps rejected it outright, and the reasons were a checklist of what not to repeat.

Too many fields No mobile access No SAP order history No personal benefit for reps

06 / What I Learned

Sequencing over software.

Whether a CRM transformation survives contact with real constraints comes down to how you sequence process and change management, far more than which features make the final cut. Get the order right, and adoption follows. Get it wrong, and the best feature list still fails.
